# KeyMaster Stage 1 — Original Studies (Making Music)

Two tiny **original** KeyMaster studies, authored for this project. They are
inspired by general public-domain *principles* (stepwise Baroque motion;
Classical question-and-answer phrasing) — **no specific piece is copied**, and no
modern edition, syllabus, or competitor material is used. Provenance is owned by
the project. Both are beginner-safe: right hand only, C five-finger position,
white keys, no reading required (listen-and-play / call-and-response).

Pitches are given as MIDI + name; "beat" = one steady pulse (no scored timing).

## Study 1 — "Morning Steps" (stepwise line)

Principle (inspiration only): even stepwise motion and a calm return — the kind of
smooth, conjunct line common in Baroque teaching pieces. Original material.

- Position: RH C-position, thumb on Middle C (60).
- Notes (one per beat): C D E F G F E D C
  `[60, 62, 64, 65, 67, 65, 64, 62, 60]`
- Fingering: 1 2 3 4 5 4 3 2 1
- Teaches: evenness, legato, finger independence, a line that *goes somewhere and
  comes home*.
- Jack (Warm Precision): "Listen first — a line that climbs, then settles home.
  Play it evenly, and let the hand stay relaxed."
- Provenance: Original KeyMaster study, inspired by stepwise Baroque motion.

## Study 2 — "Question and Answer" (antecedent / consequent)

Principle (inspiration only): a phrase that *asks* (rises, stays open) answered by
a phrase that *closes* (falls, comes to rest) — the antecedent/consequent shape
heard throughout the Classical style. Original material.

- Position: RH C-position.
- Question (open): C D E G   `[60, 62, 64, 67]`  — ends up high, unresolved.
- Answer (closed): G F E C   `[67, 65, 64, 60]`  — falls home to C, at rest.
- Fingering: Q 1 2 3 5 · A 5 4 3 1
- Teaches: phrase shape, call-and-response, the feeling of tension and resolution
  without any theory vocabulary yet.
- Jack: "Hear the question — it rises and waits. Now the answer comes down and
  rests on home. Play the question, then give its answer."
- Provenance: Original KeyMaster study, inspired by Classical question-and-answer
  phrasing.

## Stage 1 (Making Music) content ladder (target)

listen-and-play two-note pattern → low/high musical echo → three-note answer →
four-beat pulse → tiny B-major hand-shape moment → first soft chord (C–E–G as a
shape) → call-and-response phrase → "Morning Steps" → "Question and Answer".
Not reading-heavy — reading becomes central in Stage 2.

## Study 3 — "A Little Tune" (singable melody) — added rc2-111

Principle (inspiration only): a small, singable melodic gesture that dips and
returns — the kind of simple tuneful phrase common in Classical teaching pieces.
Original material; no piece copied.

- Position: RH C-position.
- Notes: E D C D E G   `[64, 62, 60, 62, 64, 67]`
- Fingering: 3 2 1 2 3 5
- Teaches: melodic shape and phrasing beyond scales/arpeggios; the feeling of a
  tune that goes somewhere and lifts at the end.
- Provenance: Original KeyMaster study, inspired by simple Classical melodic phrasing.

## rc2-112 — "Bring it back" retrieval chapter (not studies, but recall)

Three recall steps train retrieval rather than presenting new material:
- `recall-register` — low/high from memory (no marker, no demo). MIDI: learner-chosen.
- `recall-middlec` — Middle C unaided (no marker). MIDI: [60].
- `recall-echo` — echo a heard 3-note pattern unaided. MIDI: [60, 64, 62] (C E D), original.
Provenance for the echo pattern: original KeyMaster pattern (trivial 3-note motif).
