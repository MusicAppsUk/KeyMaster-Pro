# KeyMaster PRO — Course Opening & First-Lesson Research

Product record for the Course opening and first-lesson tone. These became strategic
product issues (not minor UI polish) after rc2-61. This document summarises a benchmark
research pass on the leading consumer piano-learning apps and sets KeyMaster PRO's own
position.

**Note on sources:** all competitor descriptions below are paraphrased from public reviews
and product pages (source URLs listed at the end). No competitor text, artwork, screenshots,
wording, colours, layout, or branded features are copied. They are studied only as a
standard of polish, onboarding, and emotional engagement — never imitated.

---

## What the benchmarks do well

### Skoove
- Recognises notes through microphone, Bluetooth, or MIDI, using note-detection to give
  real-time feedback; works with acoustic pianos via the device mic.
- Adds voice-over explanations and instructional videos alongside an improved sheet display.
- Reviewers single out its **technique depth** — scales, hand independence, finger agility,
  articulation — as what sets it apart, and praise that it teaches *how to practise*
  (hands separately, then together, then build speed slowly). This "how to learn" framing is
  the closest market analogue to a tutor model.

### Simply Piano
- Widely described as having the most engaging onboarding of any piano app: it asks the
  learner's level and a goal, and sets up a named profile, then gets them playing fast.
- Dead-simple feedback loop: correct notes turn green, wrong turn red and wait for a retry;
  a "Wait Mode" pauses until the correct note is played. The whole experience is built
  around momentum.
- Its weakness is instructive: it is microphone-first and **cannot see the hands**, so it
  cannot correct posture, fingering, or technique.

### Flowkey
- Setup takes minutes: it asks beginner/intermediate and which piano (MIDI or acoustic),
  then drops the learner straight into the first lesson.
- Signature screen is a clean split view — a video of the hands plus sheet music scrolling
  in sync, with notes highlighted in real time. Within ~15 minutes the learner is playing
  something recognisable; that immediate musical payoff is the hook.
- Strong practice tools: Wait Mode, section loop, slow-down, one-hand practice.
- Weakness, again instructive: it is often **too forgiving** — wrong rhythm or poor
  technique, and it can move on as if nothing's wrong — and is light on deep theory,
  technique, and classical depth.

## What KeyMaster PRO should learn (without copying)

- **The momentum loop:** greet → one tap → immediately doing something musical. The first
  interaction should produce a musical idea, not just "a sound."
- **Wait-for-input, patiently:** waiting for the right note (our gate already does this) is
  gentler and more premium than pass/fail.
- **Live, honest feedback on every note.** Our evaluator already gives real feedback — lean
  into that rather than being forgiving.
- **One focal point per screen, generous space, a single confident primary action, subtle
  progress** (not a scoreboard), and colour as a feedback language (correct glows, error
  guides).
- **Voice/guidance that arrives like a tutor**, folded into the first natural tap.

## What KeyMaster PRO must NOT copy

Layout, artwork, wording, colours, lesson flow, song content, app identity, the
subscription-first model, or any branded feature. The benchmarks are a quality bar for
polish and emotional engagement only.

## Why KeyMaster PRO is MIDI-first (for now)

Every benchmark offers both microphone (acoustic) and MIDI, and **every review agrees MIDI
is the reliable path**: the mic mishears octaves, drops notes on two-hand chords, struggles
with soft/hesitant playing, and is thrown by room noise — producing demoralising false
"wrong note" readings. Even the app that spent years tuning mic detection is only near-perfect
in quiet rooms.

For KeyMaster PRO this means **MIDI-first is a quality choice, not a deficiency.** Note data
is exact, so the tutor can give precise, honest feedback (Recognition Latency / Pattern
Fluency, Resilience Index) with no false negatives. The framing to the learner is confident,
never apologetic: *MIDI gives the tutor exact note data for precise teaching.* On-screen input
is the always-available companion.

## Why acoustic / microphone input belongs on the roadmap

Eventually KeyMaster PRO should not be limited to instruments with MIDI. Acoustic recognition
is a substantial, dedicated engine (real-time, ideally polyphonic, low-latency, noise-robust),
so it is planned as a **later additive mode** — a second input path — with MIDI/on-screen
remaining the precise default. It is sequenced *after* the premium Course opening, first-lesson
tone, and in-Course musicianship work. (See ROADMAP.md.)

## How KeyMaster PRO differentiates

KeyMaster PRO is not a song-following app. Its position:

- **The KeyMaster PRO Course at the centre**; masterclasses are specialist practice rooms.
- **Warm Precision Tutor Model** — warmth for safety, precision for progress.
- **Continuous Learning** — the tutor remembers and resumes.
- **Musicianship, not just song-following** — serious scales, reading, chords, and
  sight-reading foundations are the spine, exactly the depth the song-first market is thin on.
- **B-major ergonomic pathway** as the first real key.
- **Premium ownership model**, no subscription-first feel, ad-free.
- **MIDI/on-screen first** for accuracy and low false-feedback risk; acoustic later.

The goal is not to imitate the market. It is to compete with it — by wrapping genuine
musicianship depth in a first impression as polished and emotionally inviting as the leaders'.
